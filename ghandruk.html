Top Wonder of Nepal- Ghandruk

Given the vast landmass of Nepal, the great artist Nature has too much talent to elaborate. In north, south, east or west of Nepal, mountains, rivers, trees, stones…, no landforms can prevent the marvelous creation from Nature. Far ancient ages from millions years, this writer and painter never stop to mold the diversity on the paradise country, each natural wonders we get shocked just like a usual work it practices every day. To demonstrate the genius, the Nature gives us abundant and beautiful new looks of Nepal in every season. Misted mountains and emerald cliffs, ancient fern-ridden forests and cascading waterfalls. You’re likely to find any sort of wild path you’re willing to tread in Nepal’s manifold lands rife with majesty. At times you’ll feel you’ve stumbled into a fantasy. And perhaps you have: your own. To navigate the Land full of nature’s blessing is easy, but to choose your path when time is shorter than your desire—that’s an overwhelming prospect. 

A fairy-tale blanket of white clouds hovering over an invisible, mountain-ringed Annuparna, Ghandruk take us to the altitude of 2000 m from the sea level which is a mountain village, north-west to Pokhara, in the Modi Khola Valley. Ghandruk is famous in trekking route in Nepal as Gurung village which is a major recruitment centre for the famous Gurkha soldiers and for the natural diversity of pristine forests and snow-capped peaks. It is the gateway of trekking trail at Annapurna region as there is nothing quite as spectacular as seeing the sun rise over the Annapurna mountain in Nepal. Ghandruk is an absolute place for trekking with the most paranormic view. The Ghandruk Trek (sometimes also referred to as the Ghandrung Trek) is a great trekking option for those who are in Nepal for a shorter time or want to combine a relatively short trek with other activities in Nepal. The trek is typically done in 3 or 4 days.
